Title of Recipe

Prep Time: 10 minutes

Cook Time: 30 minutes

Total Time: 40 minutes

Relative Cost: $$/$$$$$

Servings: 2

My favorite protein to eat is chicken, and I LOVE combining it with lemon. My mom used to make me this and put it in my lunch, and ever since I've been obsessed. I thought fried chicken was hard to make, but once I started doing it myself, it's now a staple in my meal planning. I hope you enjoy this as much as we do!

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup seasoned panko
  • 2 small chicken breasts
  • 1 tsp seasonings: paprika, salt, pepper, garlic powder, parsley flakes
  • 1 tbsp lemon pepper seasoning
  • 1 lemon

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees.
  2. Create a dipping station with three containers containing: flour, egg, and panko. Add seasonings to the flour mixture, whisk the eggs, and add lemon pepper seasoning to the panko.
  3. Pound out your chicken breasts to be about 1 inch thick.
  4. Heat 1/2 in vegetable oil in a medium-sized skillet on medium heat
  5. Coat the chicken breasts in flour, then dip in the egg, and coat throroughly in the panko. Repeat for the other breast.
  6. Once the oil is hot, place the first breast in the pan with tongs.
  7. Cook both sides until golden brown. Repeat with the other chicken breast.
  8. Place the chicken on a pan with a wire rack and cook in the oven for 15 minutes, or until cooked through.
  9. Garnish with lemon and serve with your favorite veggie side.
  10. Enjoy! ðŸ˜‹

Tips

  • Add a little grated parmeasan to the Panko mixture to make it cheesy!
  • We like to enjoy this with sauteed asperagus or brussel sprouts and mashed potatoes
  • The more seasoning goes in your flour, the more flavorful it'll be!
  • If your chicken breasts are really thick, you can cut them in half.
  • Also try enjoying this cut into strips in a salad! Maybe even try it with our Chickpea Salad Recpe!
