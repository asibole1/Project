Title of Recipe

Prep Time: 3 minutes

Cook Time: 15 minutes

Total Time: 18 minutes

Relative Cost: $/$$$$$

Servings: 1

This recipe goes out to my mom, Susan, who is a queen of breakfasts. The first type of food I learned how to make is breakfast foods, and the first more complex thing I ever made was an over medium egg. Or, as she would call them when I was little "soft eggs". Imagine my surprise when a waitress looked at me funny when I ordered "soft eggs" in the restaurant - haha! Either way, this recipe is for a delicious egg sandwich that is 100x better than a McMuffin from McDonald's, although it is inspired by it.

Ingredients:

  • 1 English Muffin
  • 1 Large Egg
  • 1 Slice of Tomato
  • 1/2 Tbsp Salted Butter
  • 1/8 Cup Shredded Cheese
  • Seasonings to Taste: Salt, Pepper, Garlic Powder, Parsley

Instructions:

  1. Place a small pan on medium-low heat and add butter.
  2. Once butter is melted, crack the egg into the pan.
  3. Add your seasonings.
  4. Put english muffin into a toaster (to your preferred crispiness)
  5. Once the entire bottom of the egg is cooked and it is beginning to cook on the edges, flip the egg over carefully either with a spatula, or if you're fancy, in the air.
  6. Take the english muffin out of the toaster and add one slice of tomato. Season the tomato if you'd like.
  7. Once the egg whites are fully cooked, slide the egg onto the english muffin.
  8. Add shreded cheese on top of the egg.
  9. Place the top of the english muffin on top.
  10. Enjoy!

Tips

  • You can add a cooked sausage patty or warmed ham to add protein!
  • If you don't like the yolk, you can break the yolk in step 3 to make sure it cooks.
