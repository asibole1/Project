Chickpea Salad

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Marinating Time: 6 hours

Relative Cost: $$/$$$$$

Servings: 6

I learned this recipe from Plant Based RD Blog and give full credit to the author. This salad mixture was intended to be served on a wrap or pita but I love it as a salad for lunch. I will make one batch of it at the beginning of the week and eat it all week for lunch - it just doesn't get old! 10/10 recommend as a healthy, fresh summer lunch!

Ingredients:

  • 2, 15-oz cans of Chickpease, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1/4 red onion, diced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh basil, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • 2 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp maple syrup
  • 1/2 tsp each: dried oregano, dried thyme, dried basil, salt

Instructions:

  1. To a mixing bowl, add chickpeas, cucumbers, tomatoes, onion, parsley, and basil.
  2. Sprinkle with salt to taste.
  3. In a separate jar (or small blender), add mustard, syrup, apple cider vinegar, garlic, and fresh herbs and mix well.
  4. Toss the chickpea salad together, making sure it's coated with the dressing and then cover the bowl and place in the fridge, stirring occasionally.
  5. For best results, serve at least 6 hours later.

Tips

  • This is a great opportunity to use any leftover veggies or herbs you don't know what to do with. Experiment adding anything you think will taste good! Just make sure to chop everything super finely.
  • I like to make the dressing in a NutriBullet so that it gets nice and mixed up. I recommend this to avoid separation of the mixture.
  • I think this tastes best when marinated overnight, so I make it a day before I intend to eat it for lunch.
  • Try adding grilled chicken for additional protein!
  • You can also make a wrap out of this by putting it on a tortilla or pita bread and using guac or hummus!
