Mini Cheesecakes

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Cook Time: 18 minutes

Total Time: 33 minutes

Relative Cost: $$/$$$$$

Okay, I don't know about you but I LOVE cheesecake. However, I feel like every time I get a slice, I just can't finish it. So, as I expand in my baking abilities, I thought, "I wonder if I can make tiny cheesecakes." Sure enough, that is a thing that exists! I adapted a recipe from Live Well Bake Often into a mini muffin pan to make them EXTRA tiny. This version is bite-sized, adorable, and delicious. Plus it's super easy to make! Enjoy!

Ingredients:

For the Crust:

  • 6 graham crackers
  • 2 tbsp butter, melted
  • 1.5 tbsp sugar

For the Filling:

  • 8 ounces of cream cheese, room temp
  • 3 tbsp sour cream, room temp
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 large egg, room temp

For the Sauce:

  • 1 cup fresh or frozen fruit of your choice
  • 1/2 lemon, juiced
  • 1/4 cup sugar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 325 degrees.
  2. Blend or crush graham crackers into a powder and add butter and sugar until moistened.
  3. Add the graham cracker mixture to the bottom of a mini cupcake pan, lined with cupcake liners. It should be about 1/2 cm thick.
  4. Bake the crusts for 5 minutes.
  5. In a bowl and hand mixer (or stand mixer), combine cream cheese and sour cream until smooth. Add usgar and vanilla extract and mix till fully combined.
  6. Mix in the egg on low speed until just combined.
  7. Evenly distribute the batter into the liners filled all the way to the top.
  8. Bake for 20 minutes or until the tops of the cheesecakes are set.
  9. Remove from the oven and cool for 1 hour.
  10. Remove the liners from the pan and chill overnight in an airtight container.
  11. As the cheesecakes are chilling, put your fruit in a small sauce pot on medium-low heat. Squeeze 1/2 lemon into the pan and add sugar.
  12. Put a lid on the pot and let the fruit warm up. When you start to see condensation on the lid, open the lid and mash the fruit with a potato masher until it's at your desired smoothness (I like some fruit chunks in it myself!).
  13. Let the mixture simmer about 10-15 minutes or until it reaches desired thickness.
  14. When ready to eat, remove the cupcake liner and spoon a small amount of sauce on top.
  15. Enjoy! ðŸ˜‹

Tips

  • If you just can't wait to eat them, you can enjoy them at room temp - but it will be better chilled, trust me!
  • If you are impatient and don't get the ingredients out in time, you can microwave the cream cheese and the sour cream for 15 seconds to soften them.
  • It's okay if the tops crack in the oven, it just means that you might've overmixed the batter. It'll still taste delicious!
  • Frozen berries work just as well as fresh for the jam!
  • To make chocolate cheesecakes, you can add a little extra sugar and some baking cocoa to the batter.
  • This goes great with homemade whipped cream!
  • I don't take them out of the wrappers to store them so that they don't stick together.
  • WARNING: Be careful, these are super addictive and you'll eat them like chips if you don't restrain yourself.
